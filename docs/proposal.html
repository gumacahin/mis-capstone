What is the setting and history behind this project?
The project is focused on developing a web-based application called “WSIDT” for the University of the Philippines Open University (UPOU) community to provide an efficient and beneficial task-management and productivity-tracking tool. Currently, there are task-managing and productivity-tracking applications available in the market, but they fail to meet the feature requirements prioritized by the UPOU faculty and staff for effective planning and productivity tracking. The project aims to propose a potential solution to address the feature requirements that the majority of the intended users need, in order to fill the gap and provide a system that can meet their requirements.
What is the problem to be addressed?
The problem to be addressed is the absence of a task-management and productivity-tracking tool that meets the specific feature requirements prioritized by the UPOU community. This lack of a suitable tool can result in inefficient planning and reduced productivity. By developing an application that fulfills these feature needs, the project aims to provide the UPOU community with an effective tool to improve productivity, planning, and overall efficiency.
What are some current approaches to this problem?
The current approach is the traditional pen-and-paper, spreadsheet applications and the use off available task-management and productivity-tracking applications.
  • Google Sheets Template
  • Todist.com
  • Trello.com
Why is this problem worth solving or worth solving better?
The problem of the lack of a suitable task-management and productivity-tracking tool with the needed feature set is worth solving because it directly impacts the productivity and efficiency of the UPOU community. By providing a tool that meets their specific requirements, it has the potential to improve the quality of work output and reduce the time spent on manual tracking and management of tasks. This can ultimately lead to improved performance and productivity in the long run. Additionally, a web-based application like WSIDT can offer more accessibility and convenience to the users, as they can access it anytime and anywhere. Thus, solving this problem can lead to significant benefits for both the UPOU community and the organization as a whole.
How will this product be better than previous approaches?
Better fitting feature set: By introducing features that are not currently available in the market but are prioritized by the UPOU community, the application can offer users a unique value proposition.
Improved maintainability, scalability, and security: While the functionality of the new product may be similar to existing products, it can still surpass previous approaches by enhancing aspects such as maintainability, scalability, and security.
Our system will have similar functionality, but it is specifically aimed at the UPOU community, which is not adequately served by existing systems.

What are the main risks of this project?
  1. With the availability of various high production value tools, there is potential for the low uptake of the developed application. Success relies heavily on the usability and user satisfaction with regards to the design and performance of the web application.
  2. There are significant technical difficulties in building a web site and web application. A one person team developing the application will be a risk because there is potential for development to stall as other life events could get in the way of development.
  3. The schedule for this project is very short. We will manage this by planning a conservatively scoped functional core and series of functional enhancements that can be individually slipped to later releases if needed.
What are the main rewards if this project succeeds?
If I accomplish the elements of my plan, the application has the potential to increase the productivity of members of the UPOU productivity and increase the overall employee and student satisfaction of employees and students. There is also potential for further studies of how well-fitting information system could improve various aspects of the UPOU community.
